# 0.125.0 — 2026-07-09

**Your Team Hub, as an app.** A brain can now designate one published mini-app
as its Team Hub: members visiting `/team` see that app rendered full-screen —
your team's own design and content — while the platform keeps everything that
must stay core: the member token gate, the live Team Chat, and the in-place
briefing reader. Hub content and design now update by publishing the app
(minutes, by you or your agent) instead of waiting for a platform release.

## Highlights

- **Designate a hub app.** Team admin → "Hub app": pick any published mini-app
  and members get it as their hub. Pick "Built-in hub" to revert. Designation
  makes the app's share team-members-only automatically.

- **A thin, host-mediated app SDK.** Hub apps stay fully sandboxed (no network,
  no cookies, no tokens) and talk to the platform through three enumerated
  calls: `host.hub.get()` (the same member-safe data the built-in hub shows —
  site name, member name, briefings, live stats), `host.hub.openChat()`, and
  `host.hub.openBriefing()`. Chat and the reader remain platform surfaces the
  app can open but never embed or intercept. Authoring contract:
  `docs/team-hub-app-sdk.md`.

- **The built-in hub is the safety net.** If the designated app is unpublished,
  its share is revoked, its build breaks, or the bundle fails to boot, members
  get the built-in hub — never a broken page. Failures are detected end-to-end,
  including bundles that fetch but never start.

- **Audited like everything else.** Members reach the hub app through their
  team token; membership is re-checked on every brokered call and every access
  is logged per member, exactly as with team shares today.

## Also

- Opening Team Chat or a briefing from a hub app keeps the app mounted — "back
  to the hub" restores it instantly with scroll and state intact.
- The bridge protocol and the `@host` runtime are covered by a drift-tripwire
  test so the two can't silently diverge.
