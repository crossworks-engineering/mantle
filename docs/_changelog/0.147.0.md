# v0.147.0 — The team workspace's Pages, brought to owner polish

The `/team` Pages section now looks and behaves like the owner's own Pages
pane. Cards give way to a **collapsible sub-page tree** over exactly the pages
shared with the team — expand a page to see its shared children, nested the
same way you organised them. The header gains the same **search, sort, and
tag filter** the owner screen has, and every scroll area in the member
workspace switches to the thin, styled scrollbar used throughout the app.

## What changed

- **Collapsible tree instead of cards.** Shared pages render as compact
  icon-and-title rows with expand chevrons, rebuilding the sub-page hierarchy
  over the shared subset — a page whose parent isn't shared simply appears at
  the top level, so nothing is ever hidden.
- **Search, sort, and tags.** The same controls as the owner Pages pane:
  full-text search, the four sort orders, and a tag filter scoped to the tags
  actually present on shared pages. A search or an active tag switches to a
  flat, paginated list — exactly as the owner screen does.
- **No owner actions.** This is a reader: no New, no drag-to-reparent, no
  delete. Members browse and open; the share surface stays the only door to
  content.
- **Thin scrollbars everywhere in `/team`.** Section lists, the section nav,
  the overview, and the shared-folder chips all adopt the app's thin
  scrollbar, matching the owner workspace.
- The other member sections (Notes, Tables, Apps, Tasks, Events, Files) keep
  their card lists and gain the tag filter for free.
